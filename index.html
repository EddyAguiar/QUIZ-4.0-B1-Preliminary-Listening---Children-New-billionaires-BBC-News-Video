🎬 BBC NEWS VIDEO

📌 Meet Thomas (15, app developer), Kiowa (7, inventor), and students from the Incubator School. Listen for their ideas, advantages, and future dreams.

🔍 Before you watch: Predict the theme

You will see young people who are already entrepreneurs or want to become one. Think about:

  • What kind of products or services do children create?
  • Why might kids have a special advantage in business?
  • What does "being your own boss" mean to a seven‑year‑old?

⚡ Activate your mind: listen for names, technologies (apps, 3D printing), and school projects.

🎯 Focus while listening

  • Thomas (15): What does he create? (mobile apps / Google Glass). What summer project is he working on? (3D printing patent, faster printing).
  • Incubator School: What is its main goal? (teach entrepreneurship from age 11). What do kids learn? (advertising, profit‑sharing, starting companies).
  • 12‑year‑old students: Why do they think kids are more creative? (not boxed in by the world).
  • Kiowa (7): What does she like about being an entrepreneur? (freedom, not following orders). What is her future dream? (zoologist).
